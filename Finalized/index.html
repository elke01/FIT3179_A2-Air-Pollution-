A Visual Journey Through Air Quality and Health

Air pollution continues to be a major global health challenge to both human well-being and the environment. In light of this, let's have an in-depth exploration of air quality data, greenhouse gas emissions, and the far-reaching consequences of pollution to better address the risks posed by poor air quality.

Risk of Air Pollution

Ever wondered how much impact air pollution truly has on global health?

Air Pollution contributed to as much as 23% out of all deaths between 2000 and 2019.

Although a slight reductions have been observed during this 19 years period, this trend still underscores the significant health concern that air pollution maintain to place on the global population.

Gas Emissions from Land Use and Forestry

Land Use Land-Use Change and Forestry (LULUCF) emissions has been contributing significantly to climate change.

The largest source of LULUCF emissions comes from deforestation, primarily in South-East Asia, Africa, and Latin America.

The primary greenhouse gases released through LULUCF activities is carbon dioxide (CO2).
